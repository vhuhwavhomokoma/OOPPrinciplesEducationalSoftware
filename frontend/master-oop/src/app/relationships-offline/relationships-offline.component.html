Offline Activities

This activity will help increase your understanding of the relationships between different classes in OOP

Object Interaction Role-Play:
1. Divide students into small groups. Each group will be assigned a real-world system, such as a restaurant (e.g., waiters, customers, tables, and orders) or a hospital (e.g., doctors, nurses, patients, and rooms).

2. Students will play the roles of these objects and act out their interactions, showcasing the different relationships (Association, Aggregation, etc).

3. After the role-play, ask each group to explain how the relationships they portrayed map to OOP concepts.

Real-World Application

Demonstrate how OOP relationships are applied in real-world systems to help students connect abstract concepts to practical applications.

Examples of Real-world applications:

1. Association: A social media app where users follow each other.
2. Aggregation: A music playlist containing songs. The songs can exist even without the playlist.

Group Discussion

Identify real-world applications that utilize these relationships (Association, Aggregation, Composition, Inheritance, and Dependency).
