Polymorphism
Polymorphism is one of the important features of object-oriented programming (OOP). So, let us explore what it is. The term "polymorphism" is derived from two Greek words: “poly” meaning many, and “morphism” meaning forms. When we combine these, we get the concept of many forms. This indicates that a single entity can take on different representations.

In practical terms, polymorphism allows a function or method to operate in multiple ways depending on the context. The functionality of this feature resembles its name, as one thing can be represented in various forms.
Example of Polymorphism
Consider a simple example involving a person. Depending on the setting, this person behaves differently:
A Person
In a shopping mall: They act as a customer
On a bus: They behave as a passenger
In school: They behave as a student
At home: They behave as a daughter/son
