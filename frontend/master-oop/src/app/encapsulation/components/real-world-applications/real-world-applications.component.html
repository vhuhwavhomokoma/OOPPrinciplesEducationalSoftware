Real-World Applications of Encapsulation Understanding the Basics
Real-life Analogy:

Think of a coffee machine. You press buttons (methods) to brew coffee, but you can’t see how the coffee is made inside (data). The internal workings (like water pressure or heating elements) are hidden, and only the buttons are accessible.

Other Examples:
  • ATM Machine:

    You can only access your balance or withdraw money through specific functions (methods), but the bank’s internal system (data) is hidden.

  • Car:

    You steer, accelerate, or brake (methods), but you don’t touch the engine directly (data is encapsulated).
